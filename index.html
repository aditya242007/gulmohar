❖ Common Name: Gulmohar Tree

❖ Scientific Name: Delonix regia

❖ Classification:

Kingdom: Plantae

Family: Fabaceae

Order: Fabales

Mode of Propagation

It is propagated by seeds and cuttings. Seeds are collected, soaked in warm water, and planted in warm soil. Semi-hardwood cuttings are also used for propagation, especially for the yellow-flowering variety.

Uses

Besides being an ornamental tree, Gulmohar provides shade, produces water-soluble gum for tablet manufacturing, and its seeds are used for making beads and Pangam Oil for tanning.

Soil Needed

Prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils with neutral to slightly acidic pH. It is drought-tolerant once established but thrives best in moist soils.

Life Span

Gulmohar has a relatively short lifespan of about 30 to 50 years, but it grows rapidly and reaches maturity within a few years.

Diseases and Pests

  • Pests: Common pests include aphids, caterpillars, and borers.
  • Diseases: Susceptible to root rot if overwatered and prone to fungal infections like powdery mildew and leaf spots in humid conditions.

Medical Uses

  • Antimicrobial: Bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial properties.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Bark decoctions treat inflammatory conditions in joints and muscles.
  • Antidiabetic: Used in traditional remedies to manage blood sugar levels.
  • Wound Healing: Leaf paste is applied to wounds to aid healing and prevent infection.
